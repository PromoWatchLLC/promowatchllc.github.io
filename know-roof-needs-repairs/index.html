  • How to Know if Your Roof Needs Repairs

    The roof is an important part of any home. It adds aesthetic value and most of all; it keeps the house protected. However, the roof also happens to be the most ignored component in a house. Many home owners fail to identify signs of trouble with their roofs, which ultimately leads to an expensive roof replacement.
    In order to prevent such an expense, it is best that you, as a home owner, learn to identify signs of roof trouble. Identifying roof trouble can help you get an affordable repair done on time, instead of having to replace the whole thing.Here are some of the most common signs of roof trouble and how to identify them.

    Weather related damage

    Harsh weather is one common factor involved in roof damage, especially the winter season. Snow loads and ice damming can cause a lot of problems for your roof. Similarly, if you live in a windy location, then youâll need to start taking a look at your shingles. If the winds are strong enough where you live, then they can pull the shingles right off. So, keep an eye out for bare patches right after a storm.

    Age

    If you have an asphalt shingle roof, then youâre good for about 20 to 25 years. However, this will depend on other factors as well. For instance, a roof will last you longer if it was installed after removing the old one, leaving a single layer of shingles and if it has proper ventilation. On the other hand, if the roof was installed over an existing layer and has crossed the 20 year range, then you will, most likely, need repairs.

    Shingles

    If your shingles start curling or buckling, then thatâs a sign that you need roof repairs. To check this, just take a look at the slopes that receive direct sunlight. This will help you look for signs of buckling and curling. Buckling or curling is a typical indicator of shingle age; it means that the shingles have crossed their life expectancy.However, curled or buckled shingles might also indicate a defective roof. In this case, it is best to call an expert to confirm things.

    Light passing through the roof
    If you start noticing light passing through the roofs, then this could mean trouble. To confirm, just take a walk on the roof. If the roof is in bad shape, you will notice that it will exhibit a bouncy or spongy feel while you walk on it. What this means is that the decking at the base has become weak due to moisture. You will also have to check the insulation for presence of moisture.
